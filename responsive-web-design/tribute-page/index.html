Professional Sports

A history of the Big 5 sports leagues in North America

Here's a timeline of professional sports leagues in the United States. Let us begin:

  • 1870 - Chicago Cubs are established
  • 1871 -
    • Boston Braves are established and join the newly established National Association of Professional Base Ball Players
    • Chicago Cubs go in hiatus due to the Great Chicago Fire
  • 1874 - Chicago Cubs resume operations
  • 1876 - National League is established (Chicago Cubs and Boston Braves join the new league)
  • 1878 - Providence Grays (NL) are established
  • 1879 - Buffalo Bisons (NL) are established
  • 1881 - Detroit Wolverines (NL) are established
  • 1882 - American Association is established with six charter members: Baltimore Orioles, Cincinnati Red Stockings, Louisville Colonels, Philadelphia Athletics, Pittsburgh Alleghenys, and St. Louis Brown Stockings
  • 1883 -
    • New York Giants (NL) and Philadelphia Phillies (NL) are established
    • New York Metropolitans join the AA
  • 1884 -
    • Union Association is established but only lasts for one year due to lopsided talent distribution, instability - four franchises folded during the season, forcing the league to scramble to replace them with three teams from lower leagues and one new team, while Chicago moved to Pittsburgh mid-season - and a poorly drafted schedule
    • Brooklyn Dodgers (AA) are established
  • 1885 - St. Louis Maroons (UA) join the NL
  • 1886 -
    • Buffalo Bisons are transferred to minor league baseball
    • Providence Grays fold
  • 1887 -
    • St. Louis Maroons move to Indianapolis and become the Hoosiers
    • Cleveland Blues (AA) are established
  • 1888 - New York Metropolitans fold
  • 1889 -
    • Detroit Wolverines fold
    • Cleveland Blues (AA) join the NL and become the Spiders
  • 1890 -
    • Players' League is established but lasts only one season
    • Brooklyn Dodgers (AA) and Cincinnati Reds (AA) join the NL
    • Indianapolis Hoosiers fold
    • Cleveland Blues (AA) join the NL and become the Spiders
  • 1891 -
    • Boston Reds (PL) join the AA
    • Philadelphia Athletics (AA) are expelled and replaced by the PL Philadelphia Athletics
    • Brooklyn Dodgers (AA) and Cincinnati Reds (AA) join the NL
    • Washington Senators (AA) are established
    • American Association is disbanded
  • 1892 - Baltimore Orioles, Louisville Colonels, St. Louis Cardinals, and Washington Senators join the NL from the disbanded AL
  • 1894 - Western League is established with eight teams: Detroit Tigers, Grand Rapids Rippers, Kansas City Blues, Indianapolis Indians, Milwaukee Brewers, Minneapolis Millers, Sioux City Cornhuskers, and Toledo White Stockings
  • 1895 - Sioux City Cornhuskers move to Saint Paul and become the Saints
  • 1896 - Toledo White Stockings move to Columbus and become the Buckeyes
  • 1899 - Columbus Buckeyes fold and are replaced by the Buffalo Bisons
  • 1900 -
    • Baltimore Orioles, Cleveland Spiders, Louisville Colonels and Washington Senators fold
    • St. Paul Saints move to Chicago and become the White Stockings
    • Grand Rapids Rippers move to Cleveland and become the Bluebirds
    • National League "Classic Eight" lineup of teams is established: Chicago Cubs, Boston Braves, Brooklyn Dodgers, New York Giants, Philadelphia Phillies, Pittsburgh Pirates, Cincinnati Reds, and St. Louis Cardinals
  • 1901 - American League is established with eight former WL teams: Baltimore Orioles, Boston Red Sox, Chicago White Sox, Cleveland Indians, Detroit Tigers, Milwaukee Brewers, Philadelphia Athletics, and Washington Senators
  • 1902 -
    • Milwaukee Brewers move to St. Louis and become the Browns
    • American Association is established
  • 1903 -
    • National League and American League sign the National Agreement, forming Major League Baseball, cementing their cooperation as two leagues under one banner
    • Pacific Coast League is established
    • Baltimore Orioles fold and are replaced by the New York Yankees
  • 1910 - Montreal Canadiens are established and join the newly christened National Hockey Association along with the Montreal Wanderers, Ottawa Senators, and Quebec Bulldogs
  • 1912 -
    • International League is established
    • Toronto Blueshirts (NHA) are established
    • Pacific Coast Hockey Association is established with three teams: New Westminster Royals, Vancouver Millionaires, and Victoria Senators
  • 1914 -
    • Federal League is established as a "third major league" with its own eight teams: Baltimore Terrapins, Brooklyn Tip-Tops, Buffalo Blues, Chicago Whales, Indianapolis Hoosiers, Kansas City Packers, Pittsburgh Rebels, and St. Louis Terriers
    • New Westminster Royals move to Portland and become the Rosebuds
  • 1915 -
    • Indianapolis Hoosiers move to Newark and become the Peppers
    • Seattle Metropolitans (PCHA) are established
  • 1916 -
    • FL suspends operations and eventually folds
    • Victoria Aristocrats move to Spokane and become the Canaries but only last one season
  • 1917 - NHA suspends operations (the association would never return) and is replaced by the National Hockey League with three of its former teams: Montreal Canadiens, Montreal Wanderers (who withdraw from the league due to their arena burning down), Ottawa Senators, and the newly enfranchised Toronto Arenas
  • 1919 -
    • Quebec Bulldogs join the NHL
    • Portland Rosebuds fold and are replaced by the reactivated Victoria Aristocrats
    • Toronto Arenas are permanently established as the Toronto Maple Leafs
  • 1920 -
    • American Professional Football Association is established with teams including the Akron Pros, Buffalo Bisons, Canton Bulldogs, Chicago Cardinals, Dayton Triangles, Decatur Staleys, Hammond Pros, Rochester Jeffersons, and Rock Island Independents
    • Quebec Bulldogs move to Hamilton and become the Tigers
    • Negro National League is established with eight teams, the first black baseball league to be designated a major league
  • 1921 -
    • Green Bay Packers join the APFA
    • Decatur Staleys move to Chicago
    • Western Canada Hockey League is established with four teams: Calgary Tigers, Edmonton Eskimos, Regina Capitals, and Saskatoon Sheiks
  • 1922 -
    • APFA becomes the National Football League
    • Milwaukee Badgers (NFL) are established
    • Chicago Staleys become the Bears
  • 1923 -
    • Cleveland Indians (NFL) are established
    • Eastern Colored League is established
  • 1924 -
    • Canton Bulldogs are mothballed and moved to Cleveland
    • Frankford Yellow Jackets, Kansas City Cowboys, and Milwaukee Badgers join the NFL
    • Eastern Colored League is established
    • Boston Bruins (NHL) and Montreal Maroons (NHL) are established
    • PCHA is disbanded, and two of its teams (Vancouver Maroons and Victoria Cougars) join the WCHL
  • 1925 -
    • Providence Steam Roller join the NFL
    • Canton Bulldogs rejoin the NFL
    • New York Giants (NFL) and Pottsville Maroons (NFL) are established
    • Eastern Colored League is established
    • Hamilton Tigers are terminated and replaced by the New York Americans
    • Pittsburgh Pirates (NHL) are established
    • Regina Capitals move to Portland and become the Rosebuds
  • 1926 -
    • The first American Football League is established with the Rock Island Independents jumping to the new league
    • ECL disbands midway into baseball season
    • Eastern Colored League is established
    • Western Hockey League is disbanded
    • Chicago Blackhawks (NHL), Detroit Red Wings (NHL), and New York Rangers (NHL) are established
  • 1927 -
    • Several NFL teams are axed, leaving twelve teams in the league
    • New York Yankees (AFL I) join the NFL
  • 1928 -
    • Cleveland Bulldogs move to Detroit and become the Wolverines, only to fold along with he New York NFL Yankees after the season ends
    • ECL disbands midway into baseball season
  • 1929 -
    • Pottsville Maroons move to Boston and become the Bulldogs but fold after the season ends
    • Buffalo Bisons rejoin the NFL but also fold after the season ends
    • American National League is established but lasts only one season
  • 1930 -
    • Dayton Triangles move to Brooklyn, New York and become the Dodgers
    • Portsmouth Spartans (NFL) are established
    • Pittsburgh Hockey Pirates move to Philadelphia and become the Quakers
  • 1931 -
    • Frankford Yellow Jackets fold midway through the season while Providence Steam Roller fold at the end
    • NNL disbands mid-season though its teams continue to play each other as independent teams
    • Ottawa Senators and Philadelphia Quakers suspend operations: the latter would never return
  • 1932 -
    • Boston Redskins (NFL) are established
    • East-West League is established but lasts one season
    • Negro Southern League is promoted to the majors but is relegated back to the minor leagues after the season ends
    • Ottawa Senators rejoin the NHL
  • 1933 -
    • Philadelphia Eagles (NFL) and Pittsburgh Steelers (NFL) are established
    • The second Negro National League is established
  • 1934 -
    • Portsmouth Spartans move to Detroit and become the Lions
    • Ottawa Senators move to St. Louis and become the Eagles but are disbanded after the season
  • 1937 -
    • Cleveland Rams join the NFL from the second iteration of the American Football League
    • Boston Redskins move to Washington, D.C.
    • Negro American League is established
    • National Basketball League is established
  • 1938 -
    • Sheboygan Red Skins join the NBL
    • Montreal Maroons suspend operations and would never return)
  • 1941 - Fort Wayne Zollner Pistons (NBL) are established
  • 1942 -
    • Cleveland Rams suspend operations due to World War II
    • New York Americans (NHL) suspend operations due to World War II and long-standing financial difficulties but would never return
    • Original Six era begins with the six surviving teams: Boston Bruins, Chicago Blackhawks, Detroit Red Wings, Montreal Canadiens, New York Rangers, and Toronto Maple Leafs
  • 1943 - Philadelphia Eagles and Pittsburgh Steelers merge to form the "Phil-Pitt Steagles"
  • 1944 -
    • Boston Yanks (NFL) are established
    • Cleveland Rams resume operations
    • Phil-Pitt Steagles end merger, and Philadelpiha Eagles resume operations
    • Philadelphia Steelers merge with Chicago Cardinals to form "Card-Pitt"
    • Baltimore Bullets are established
  • 1945 -
    • Card-Pitt is resplit into the Chicago Cardinals and Pittsburgh Steelers
    • Brooklyn Tigers are merged with Boston Yanks, named "The Yanks"
    • Rochester Royals join the NBL
  • 1946 -
    • Cleveland Rams move to Los Angeles
    • All-America Football Conference is established with eight teams: Brooklyn Dodgers, Buffalo Bills, Cleveland Browns, Chicago Rockets, Los Angeles Dons, Miami Seahawks (who fold after one season), New York Yankees, and San Francisco 49ers
    • American Association reaches AAA status with teams including: Columbus Red Birds, Indianapolis Indians, Kansas City Blues, Louisville Colonels, Milwaukee Brewers, Minneapolis Millers, St. Paul Saints, and Toledu Mud Hens
    • International League achieves AAA status with teams including: Baltimore Orioles, Buffalo Bisons, Jersey City Giants, Montreal Royals, Newark Bears, Rochester Red Wings, Syracuse Chiefs, and Toronto Maple Leafs
    • Basketball Association of America is established with eleven teams: Boston Celtics, Chicago Stags, Cleveland Rebels, Detroit Falcons, New York Knicks, Philadelphia Warriors, Pittsburgh Ironmen, Providence Steamrollers, St. Louis Bombers, Toronto Huskies, and Washington Capitols
    • Anderson Packers (NBL), Buffalo Bisons (NBL but moved to Moline, Illinois on Christmas Day to become the Tri-Cities Blackhawks) and Syracuse Nationals (NBL) are established
  • 1947 -
    • Baltimore Colts (AAFC) are established from the defunct Miami Seahawks
    • Brooklyn Dodgers, Cleveland Indians, and St. Louis Browns are the first MLB teams to integrate
    • Cleveland Rebels, Detroit Falcons, Pittsburgh Ironmen, and Toronto Huskies fold after the season
    • Baltimore Bullets join BAA from the American Basketball League
    • Minneapolis Lakers (NBL) are established
  • 1948 -
    • Four NBL teams (Fort Wayne Pistons, Indianapolis Jets, Minneapolis Lakers, and Rochester Royals) join the BAA
    • Denver Nuggets and Waterloo Hawks join the NBL
  • 1949 -
    • Boston Yanks fold and are replaced by the New York Bulldogs
    • Brooklyn Dodgers (AAFC) fold and are merged with the New York Yankees
    • Due to the ongoing process of integration, NAL loses its major league status while the NNL disbands. All but one of the now-former NNL teams join the NAL.
    • New York Giants (MLB) integrate
    • Indianapolis Jets and Providence Steamrollers fold after the season, the former replaced by the expansion Indianapolis Olympians
    • BAA and NBL merge to form the National Basketball Association
    • Anderson Packers, Denver Nuggets, Sheboygan Red Skins, Syracuse Nationals, Tri-Cities Blackhawks, and Waterloo Hawks join the new NBA from the NBL
  • 1950 -
    • AAFC merge with the NFL
    • New York Yankees (AAFC) fold and merge with New York Giants and New York Bulldogs (who become the Yanks)
    • Los Angeles Dons fold and merge with the Los Angeles Rams
    • Cleveland Browns, San Francisco 49ers, and Baltimore Colts move to the NFL, but the Colts fold after one season
    • Boston Braves integrate
    • Chicago Stags, Denver Nuggets, and St. Louis Bombers fold after the season
    • Anderson Packers, Sheboygan Red SKins, and Waterloo Hawks jump to the newly established National Professional Basketball League
  • 1951 -
    • New York Yanks fold after the season
    • Chicago White Sox integrate
    • Washington Capitols also fold midway into the 1950 season
    • Tri-Cities Blackhawks move to Milwaukee and become the Hawks
  • 1952 -
    • Dallas Texans (NFL) are established with the remains of the now-defunct New York Yanks but are terminated after one season (it remains the last NFL team to fold due to financial reasons)
    • Pacific Coast League (AAA) achieves the "Open" classification with teams including: Hollywood Stars, Los Angeles Angels, Oakland Oaks, Portland Beavers, Sacramento Solons, San Diego Padres, San Francisco Seals, and Seattle Rainiers
  • 1953 -
    • A second and distinct Baltimore Colts are established from the remains of the Dallas Texans
    • Boston Braves move to Milwaukee, displacing the American Association Milwaukee Brewers
    • Chicago Cubs, Philadelphia Athletics, and Pittsburgh Pirates integrate
    • Indianapolis Olympians (NBA) fold after the season
  • 1954 -
    • St. Louis Browns move to Baltimore and become the Orioles, displacing the International League team of the same name
    • Cincinnati Reds, St. Louis Cardinals, and Washington Senators integrate
    • Baltimore Bullets fold early into the season, the last NBA team to fold
  • 1954 -
    • Philadelphia Athletics move to Kansas City, displacing the American Association Kansas City Blues
    • New York Yankees integrate
    • Milwaukee Hawks move to St. Louis
  • 1957 -
    • Philadelphia Phillies integrate
    • Fort Wayne Pistons move to Detroit
    • Rochester Royals move to Cincinnati
  • 1958 -
    • Brooklyn Dodgers and New York Giants move to Los Angeles and San Francisco, respectively, displacing the PCL's Hollywood Stars, Los Angeles Angels, Oakland Oaks, and San Francisco Seals, effectively ending the PCL's bid to become a major league
    • Detroit Tigers integrate
  • 1959 - Boston Red Sox are the last MLB team to integrate
