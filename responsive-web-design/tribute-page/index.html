Professional Sports

A history of the Big 5 sports leagues in North America

Here's a timeline of professional sports leagues in the United States. Let us begin:

  • 1870 - Chicago Cubs are established
  • 1871 -
    • Boston Braves are established and join the newly established National Association of Professional Base Ball Players
    • Chicago Cubs go in hiatus due to the Great Chicago Fire
  • 1874 - Chicago Cubs resume operations
  • 1876 - National League is established (Chicago Cubs and Boston Braves join the new league)
  • 1878 - Providence Grays (NL) are established
  • 1879 - Buffalo Bisons (NL) are established
  • 1881 - Detroit Wolverines (NL) are established
  • 1882 - American Association is established with six charter members:
    1. Baltimore Orioles
    2. Cincinnati Red Stockings
    3. Louisville Colonels
    4. Philadelphia Athletics
    5. Pittsburgh Alleghenys
    6. St. Louis Brown Stockings
  • 1883 -
    • New York Giants (NL) and Philadelphia Phillies (NL) are established
    • New York Metropolitans join the AA
  • 1884 -
    • Union Association is established but only lasts for one year due to lopsided talent distribution, instability - four franchises folded during the season, forcing the league to scramble to replace them with three teams from lower leagues and one new team, while Chicago moved to Pittsburgh mid-season - and a poorly drafted schedule
    • Brooklyn Dodgers (AA) are established
  • 1885 - St. Louis Maroons (UA) join the NL
  • 1886 -
    • Buffalo Bisons are transferred to minor league baseball
    • Providence Grays cease operations
  • 1887 -
    • St. Louis Maroons move to Indianapolis and become the Hoosiers
    • Cleveland Blues (AA) are established
  • 1888 - New York Metropolitans cease operations
  • 1889 -
    • Detroit Wolverines cease operations
    • Cleveland Blues (AA) join the NL and become the Spiders
  • 1890 -
    • Players' League is established but lasts only one season
    • Brooklyn Dodgers (AA) and Cincinnati Reds (AA) join the NL
    • Indianapolis Hoosiers cease operations
    • Cleveland Blues (AA) join the NL and become the Spiders
  • 1891 -
    • Boston Reds (PL) join the AA
    • Philadelphia Athletics (AA) are expelled and replaced by the PL Philadelphia Athletics
    • Brooklyn Dodgers (AA) and Cincinnati Reds (AA) join the NL
    • Washington Senators (AA) are established
    • American Association is disbanded
  • 1892 - Baltimore Orioles, Louisville Colonels, St. Louis Cardinals, and Washington Senators join the NL from the disbanded AA
  • 1894 - Western League is established with eight teams:
    1. Detroit Tigers
    2. Grand Rapids Rippers
    3. Kansas City Blues
    4. Indianapolis Indians
    5. Milwaukee Brewers
    6. Minneapolis Millers
    7. Sioux City Cornhuskers
    8. Toledo White Stockings
  • 1895 - Sioux City Cornhuskers move to Saint Paul and become the Saints
  • 1896 - Toledo White Stockings move to Columbus and become the Buckeyes
  • 1899 - Columbus Buckeyes cease operations and are replaced by the Buffalo Bisons
  • 1900 -
    • Baltimore Orioles, Cleveland Spiders, Louisville Colonels and Washington Senators cease operations
    • St. Paul Saints move to Chicago and become the White Stockings
    • Grand Rapids Rippers move to Cleveland and become the Bluebirds
    • National League "Classic Eight" lineup of teams is established:
      1. Boston Braves
      2. Brooklyn Dodgers
      3. Chicago Cubs
      4. Cincinnati Reds
      5. New York Giants
      6. Philadelphia Phillies
      7. Pittsburgh Pirates
      8. St. Louis Cardinals
  • 1901 - American League is established with eight former WL teams:
    1. Baltimore Orioles
    2. Boston Red Sox
    3. Chicago White Sox
    4. Cleveland Indians
    5. Detroit Tigers
    6. Milwaukee Brewers
    7. Philadelphia Athletics
    8. Washington Senators
  • 1902 -
    • Milwaukee Brewers move to St. Louis and become the Browns
    • American Association is established
  • 1903 -
    • National League and American League sign the National Agreement, forming Major League Baseball, cementing their cooperation as two leagues under one banner
    • Pacific Coast League is established
    • Baltimore Orioles cease operations and are replaced by the New York Yankees
  • 1910 - Montreal Canadiens are established and join the newly christened National Hockey Association along with the Montreal Wanderers, Ottawa Senators, and Quebec Bulldogs
  • 1912 -
    • International League is established
    • Toronto Blueshirts (NHA) are established
    • Pacific Coast Hockey Association is established with three teams:
      1. New Westminster Royals
      2. Vancouver Millionaires
      3. Victoria Senators
  • 1914 -
    • Federal League is established as a "third major league" with its own eight teams:
      1. Baltimore Terrapins
      2. Brooklyn Tip-Tops
      3. Buffalo Blues
      4. Chicago Whales
      5. Indianapolis Hoosiers
      6. Kansas City Packers
      7. Pittsburgh Rebels
      8. St. Louis Terriers
    • New Westminster Royals move to Portland and become the Rosebuds
  • 1915 -
    • Indianapolis Hoosiers move to Newark and become the Peppers
    • Seattle Metropolitans (PCHA) are established
  • 1916 -
    • FL suspends operations and eventually disbands for good
    • Victoria Aristocrats move to Spokane and become the Canaries but only last one season
  • 1917 - NHA suspends operations (the association would never return) and is replaced by the National Hockey League with three of its former teams:
    1. Montreal Canadiens
    2. Montreal Wanderers (who withdraw from the league due to their arena burning down)
    3. Ottawa Senators
    4. Toronto Arenas (newly enfranchised)
  • 1919 -
    • Quebec Bulldogs join the NHL
    • Portland Rosebuds cease operations and are replaced by the reactivated Victoria Aristocrats
    • Toronto Arenas are permanently established as the Toronto Maple Leafs
  • 1920 -
    • American Professional Football Association is established with teams including the Akron Pros, Buffalo Bisons, Canton Bulldogs, Chicago Cardinals, Dayton Triangles, Decatur Staleys, Hammond Pros, Rochester Jeffersons, and Rock Island Independents
    • Quebec Bulldogs move to Hamilton and become the Tigers
    • Negro National League is established with eight teams, the first black baseball league to be designated a major league
  • 1921 -
    • Green Bay Packers join the APFA
    • Decatur Staleys move to Chicago
    • Western Canada Hockey League is established with four teams:
      1. Calgary Tigers
      2. Edmonton Eskimos
      3. Regina Capitals
      4. Saskatoon Sheiks
  • 1922 -
    • APFA becomes the National Football League
    • Milwaukee Badgers (NFL) are established
    • Chicago Staleys become the Bears
  • 1923 -
    • Cleveland Indians (NFL) are established
    • Eastern Colored League is established
  • 1924 -
    • Canton Bulldogs move to Cleveland
    • Frankford Yellow Jackets, Kansas City Cowboys, and Milwaukee Badgers join the NFL
    • Eastern Colored League is established
    • Boston Bruins (NHL) and Montreal Maroons (NHL) are established
    • PCHA is disbanded, and two of its teams (Vancouver Maroons and Victoria Cougars) join the WCHL
  • 1925 -
    • Providence Steam Roller join the NFL
    • Canton Bulldogs rejoin the NFL
    • New York Giants (NFL) and Pottsville Maroons (NFL) are established
    • Eastern Colored League is established
    • Hamilton Tigers are terminated and replaced by the New York Americans
    • Pittsburgh Pirates (NHL) are established
    • Regina Capitals move to Portland and become the Rosebuds
  • 1926 -
    • The first American Football League is established with the Rock Island Independents jumping to the new league
    • ECL disbands midway into baseball season
    • Eastern Colored League is established
    • Western Hockey League is disbanded
    • Chicago Blackhawks (NHL), Detroit Red Wings (NHL), and New York Rangers (NHL) are established
  • 1927 -
    • Several NFL teams cease operations, leaving twelve teams in the league
    • New York Yankees (AFL I) join the NFL
  • 1928 -
    • Cleveland Bulldogs move to Detroit and become the Wolverines, only to fold along with he New York NFL Yankees after the season ends
    • ECL disbands midway into baseball season
  • 1929 -
    • Pottsville Maroons move to Boston and become the Bulldogs but cease operations after the season ends
    • Buffalo Bisons rejoin the NFL but also cease operations after the season ends
    • American National League is established but lasts only one season
  • 1930 -
    • Dayton Triangles move to Brooklyn, New York and become the Dodgers
    • Portsmouth Spartans (NFL) are established
    • Pittsburgh Hockey Pirates move to Philadelphia and become the Quakers
  • 1931 -
    • Frankford Yellow Jackets cease operations midway through the season with Providence Steam Roller folding at the end
    • NNL disbands mid-season though its teams continue to play each other as independent teams
    • Ottawa Senators and Philadelphia Quakers suspend operations: the latter would never return
  • 1932 -
    • Boston Redskins (NFL) are established
    • East-West League is established but lasts one season
    • Negro Southern League is promoted to the majors but is relegated back to the minor leagues after the season ends
    • Ottawa Senators resume operations
  • 1933 -
    • Philadelphia Eagles (NFL) and Pittsburgh Steelers (NFL) are established
    • The second Negro National League is established
  • 1934 -
    • Portsmouth Spartans move to Detroit and become the Lions
    • Ottawa Senators move to St. Louis and become the Eagles but cease operations after the season
  • 1936 - American Hockey League is established with eight teams:
    1. Buffalo Bisons (who disband after eleven games)
    2. Cleveland Falcons
    3. New Haven Eagles
    4. Philadelphia Ramblers
    5. Pittsburgh Hornets
    6. Providence Reds
    7. Springfield Indians
    8. syracuse Stars
  • 1937 -
    • Cleveland Rams join the NFL from the second iteration of the American Football League
    • Boston Redskins move to Washington, D.C.
    • Negro American League is established
    • National Basketball League is established
    • Cleveland Falcons become the Barons
  • 1938 -
    • Sheboygan Red Skins join the NBL
    • Montreal Maroons suspend operations and would never return
    • Hershey Bears join the officially merged American Hockey League
  • 1939 - Indianapolis Capitals (AHL) are established
  • 1940 - Syracuse Stars move to Buffalo and become the Bisons
  • 1941 -
    • Fort Wayne Zollner Pistons (NBL) are established
    • Philadelphia Ramblers become the Rockets
    • Washington Lions (AHL) are established
  • 1942 -
    • Cleveland Rams suspend operations due to World War II
    • New York Americans (NHL) suspend operations due to World War II and long-standing financial difficulties but would never return
    • Philadelphia Rockets and Springfield Indians cease operations
    • Original Six era begins with the six surviving teams:
      1. Boston Bruins
      2. Chicago Blackhawks
      3. Detroit Red Wings
      4. Montreal Canadiens
      5. New York Rangers
      6. Toronto Maple Leafs
  • 1943 -
    • New Haven Eagles suspend operations midway into the season
    • Philadelphia Eagles and Pittsburgh Steelers merge to form the "Phil-Pitt Steagles"
    • Washington Lions suspend operations due to World War II
  • 1944 -
    • Boston Yanks (NFL) are established
    • Cleveland Rams resume operations
    • Phil-Pitt Steagles end merger, and Philadelpiha Eagles resume operations
    • Philadelphia Steelers merge with Chicago Cardinals to form "Card-Pitt"
    • Baltimore Bullets are established
    • St. Louis Flyers join the AHL
  • 1945 -
    • Card-Pitt is resplit into the Chicago Cardinals and Pittsburgh Steelers
    • Brooklyn Tigers are merged with Boston Yanks, named "The Yanks"
    • Rochester Royals join the NBL
    • New Haven Eagles resume operations
  • 1946 -
    • Cleveland Rams move to Los Angeles
    • All-America Football Conference is established with eight teams:
      1. Brooklyn Dodgers
      2. Buffalo Bills
      3. Cleveland Browns
      4. Chicago Rockets
      5. Los Angeles Dons
      6. Miami Seahawks (who cease operations after one season)
      7. New York Yankees
      8. San Francisco 49ers
    • American Association reaches AAA status with teams including:
      1. Columbus Red Birds
      2. Indianapolis Indians
      3. Kansas City Blues
      4. Louisville Colonels
      5. Milwaukee Brewers
      6. Minneapolis Millers
      7. St. Paul Saints
      8. Toledu Mud Hens
    • International League achieves AAA status with teams including:
      1. Baltimore Orioles
      2. Buffalo Bisons
      3. Jersey City Giants
      4. Montreal Royals
      5. Newark Bears
      6. Rochester Red Wings
      7. Syracuse Chiefs
      8. Toronto Maple Leafs
    • Basketball Association of America is established with eleven teams:
      1. Boston Celtics
      2. Chicago Stags
      3. Cleveland Rebels
      4. Detroit Falcons
      5. New York Knicks
      6. Philadelphia Warriors
      7. Pittsburgh Ironmen
      8. Providence Steamrollers
      9. St. Louis Bombers
      10. Toronto Huskies
      11. Washington Capitols
    • Anderson Packers (NBL), Buffalo Bisons (NBL but moved to Moline, Illinois on Christmas Day to become the Tri-Cities Blackhawks) and Syracuse Nationals (NBL) are established
    • Springfield Indians (AHL) resume operations
    • Philadelphia Rockets (AHL) are re-established
  • 1947 -
    • Baltimore Colts (AAFC) are established from the defunct Miami Seahawks
    • Brooklyn Dodgers, Cleveland Indians, and St. Louis Browns are the first MLB teams to integrate
    • Cleveland Rebels, Detroit Falcons, Pittsburgh Ironmen, and Toronto Huskies cease operations after the season
    • Baltimore Bullets join BAA from the American Basketball League
    • Minneapolis Lakers (NBL) are established
    • Washington Lions (AHL) resume operations
  • 1948 -
    • Four NBL teams (Fort Wayne Pistons, Indianapolis Jets, Minneapolis Lakers, and Rochester Royals) join the BAA
    • Denver Nuggets and Waterloo Hawks join the NBL
  • 1949 -
    • Boston Yanks cease operations and are replaced by the New York Bulldogs
    • Brooklyn Dodgers (AAFC) cease operations and are merged with the New York Yankees
    • NAL loses its major league status while the NNL disbands due to the ongoing process of integration, leading all but one of the now-former NNL teams to join the NAL
    • New York Giants (MLB) integrate
    • Indianapolis Jets and Providence Steamrollers cease operations after the season, the former replaced by the expansion Indianapolis Olympians
    • BAA and NBL merge to form the National Basketball Association
    • Anderson Packers, Denver Nuggets, Sheboygan Red Skins, Syracuse Nationals, Tri-Cities Blackhawks, and Waterloo Hawks join the new NBA from the NBL
    • Philadelphia Rockets cease operations
    • Washington Lions move to Cincinnati and become the Mohawks
  • 1950 -
    • AAFC merge with the NFL
    • New York Yankees (AAFC) cease operations and merge with New York Giants and New York Bulldogs (who become the Yanks)
    • Los Angeles Dons cease operations and merge with the Los Angeles Rams
    • Cleveland Browns, San Francisco 49ers, and Baltimore Colts move to the NFL, but the Colts cease operations after one season
    • Boston Braves integrate
    • Chicago Stags, Denver Nuggets, and St. Louis Bombers cease operations after the season
    • Anderson Packers, Sheboygan Red SKins, and Waterloo Hawks jump to the newly established National Professional Basketball League
  • 1951 -
    • New York Yanks fold after the season
    • Chicago White Sox integrate
    • NPBL is disbanded before the postseason
    • Washington Capitols fold midway into the 1951 season
    • Tri-Cities Blackhawks move to Milwaukee and become the Hawks
    • New Haven Eagles cease operations 28 games into the season
  • 1952 -
    • Dallas Texans (NFL) are established with the remains of the now-defunct New York Yanks but are terminated after one season (it remains the last NFL team to fold due to financial reasons)
    • Pacific Coast League (AAA) achieves the "Open" classification with teams including:
      1. Hollywood Stars
      2. Los Angeles Angels
      3. Oakland Oaks
      4. Portland Beavers
      5. Sacramento Solons
      6. San Diego Padres
      7. San Francisco Seals
      8. Seattle Rainiers
    • Western Hockey League is established with teams including: Calgary Stampeders, Edmonton Flyers, New Westminster Royals, Saskatoon Quakers, Seattle Bombers, Vancouver Canucks, and Victoria Cougars
    • Indianapolis Capitals cease operations
    • The Cincinnati Mohawks transfer to the International Hockey League
  • 1953 -
    • A second and distinct Baltimore Colts are established from the remains of the Dallas Texans
    • Boston Braves move to Milwaukee, displacing the American Association Milwaukee Brewers
    • Chicago Cubs, Philadelphia Athletics, and Pittsburgh Pirates integrate
    • Indianapolis Olympians (NBA) cease operations after the season
    • St. Louis Flyers cease operations
  • 1954 -
    • St. Louis Browns move to Baltimore and become the Orioles, displacing the International League team of the same name
    • Cincinnati Reds, St. Louis Cardinals, and Washington Senators integrate
    • Baltimore Bullets cease operations early into the season, the last NBA team to fold
    • Seattle Bombers (WHL) suspend operations
  • 1955 -
    • Philadelphia Athletics move to Kansas City, displacing the American Association Kansas City Blues
    • New York Yankees integrate
    • Milwaukee Hawks move to St. Louis
    • Seattle Totems (WHL) and Winnipeg Warriors (WHL) are established; Regina Regals also join but immediately move to Brandon
  • 1956 -
    • Saskatoon Quakers cease operations after the season
    • Pittsburgh Hornets move to Rochester and become the Americans
  • 1957 -
    • Philadelphia Phillies integrate
    • Fort Wayne Pistons move to Detroit
    • Rochester Royals move to Cincinnati
    • Brandon Regals move to Saskatoon and become the Quakers
  • 1958 -
    • Brooklyn Dodgers and New York Giants move to Los Angeles and San Francisco, respectively, displacing the PCL's Hollywood Stars, Los Angeles Angels, Oakland Oaks, and San Francisco Seals, effectively ending the PCL's bid to become a major league
    • Detroit Tigers integrate
    • Spokane Comets (WHL) are established
  • 1959 -
    • Boston Red Sox are the last MLB team to integrate
    • New Westminster Royals and Saskatoon Quakers cease operations for good after the season
    • Quebec Aces join the AHL
  • 1960 -
    • American Football League (AFL IV) is established with eight teams:
      1. Boston Patriots
      2. Buffalo Bills
      3. Dallas Texans
      4. Denver Broncos
      5. Houston Oilers
      6. Los Angeles Chargers
      7. New York Jets
      8. Oakland Raiders
    • Dallas Cowboys (NFL) are established
    • Chicago Cardinals move to St. Louis
    • Minneapolis Lakers move to Los Angeles
    • Portland Buckaroos (WHL) are established
  • 1961 -
    • Minnesota Vikings (NFL) are established
    • Los Angeles Chargers move to San Diego
    • Continental League is established as a major league with eight teams:
      1. Atlanta Crackers (Southern Association)
      2. Buffalo Bisons (International League)
      3. Dallas Rangers (American Association)
      4. Denver Bears (American Association)
      5. Houston Colt .45s (formerly the Buffaloes from the American Association)
      6. Minnesota Twins (merged from the American Association's Minneapolis Millers and St. Paul Saints)
      7. New York Mets (expansion team)
      8. Toronto Maple Leafs (International League)
    • Chicago Packers (NBA) are established
    • Winnipeg Warriors (WHL) suspend operations and never return
    • Victoria Cougars move to Los Angeles and become the Blades
    • San Francisco Seals (WHL) are established
    • Pittsburgh Hornets resume operations
  • 1962 -
    • Philadelphia Warriors move to San Francisco
    • Chicago Packers become the Zephyrs
    • Baltimore Clippers (AHL) are established
  • 1963 -
    • Dallas Texans move to Kansas City and become the Chiefs
    • Chicago Zephyrs move to Baltimore and become the second iteration of the Bullets
    • Syracuse Nationals move to Philadelphia and become the 76ers
    • Calgary Stampeders and Edmonton Flyers fold after the season
    • Spokane Comets move to Denver and become the Invaders
  • 1964 - Denver Invaders move to Victoria and become the Maple Leafs
  • 1965 -
    • Houston Colt .45s become the Astros
    • Denver Bears move to Anaheim and become the California Angels
  • 1966 -
    • Atlanta Falcons (NFL) and Miami Dolphins (AFL) are established
    • Atlanta Crackers become the Shamrocks
    • Chicago Bulls (NBA) are established
    • San Diego Gulls (WHL) are established
  • 1967 -
    • New Orleans Saints (NFL) are established
    • San Diego Rockets (NBA) and Seattle Supersonics (NBA) are established
    • American Basketball Association is established with eleven teams:
      1. Anaheim Amigos
      2. Dallas Chaparrals
      3. Denver Rockets
      4. Houston Mavericks
      5. Indiana Pacers
      6. Kentucky Colonels
      7. Minnesota Muskies
      8. New Jersey Americans
      9. New Orleans Buccaneers
      10. Oakland Oaks
      11. Pittsburgh Pipers
    • Victoria Maple Leafs move to Phoenix and become the Roadrunners
    • Six new teams join the NHL:
      1. California Seals (from WHL)
      2. Los Angeles Kings (displacing the WHL Los Angeles Blades)
      3. Minnesota North Stars
      4. Philadelphia Flyers
      5. Pittsburgh Penguins (displacing the AHL Pittsburgh Hornets)
      6. St. Louis Blues
  • 1968 -
    • Cincinnati Bengals (AFL) are established
    • Milwaukee Bucks (NBA) and Phoenix Suns (NBA) are established
    • St. Louis Hawks move to Atlanta
    • Minnesota Muskies move to Miami and become the Floridians
    • Pittsburgh Pipers move to Minneapolis-St. Paul, Minnesota
    • Anaheim Amigos move to Los Angeles and become the Stars
    • New Jersey Americans move to Long Island, New York and become the Nets
    • Denver Spurs (WHL) are established
    • North American Soccer League is established from 17 teams of the merged National Professional Soccer League and United Soccer Association:
      1. Atlanta Chiefs (NPSL)
      2. Baltimore Bays (NPSL)
      3. Boston Beacons (USA)
      4. Chicago Mustangs (USA)
      5. Cleveland Stokers (USA)
      6. Dallas Tornado (USA)
      7. Detroit Cougars (USA)
      8. Houston Stars (USA)
      9. Kansas City Spurs (NPSL from Chicago)
      10. Los Angeles Wolves (USA)
      11. New York Generals (NPSL)
      12. Oakland Clippers (NPSL)
      13. St. Louis Stars (NPSL)
      14. San Diego Toros (NPSL from Los Angeles)
      15. Toronto Falcons (NPSL)
      16. Vancouver Royals (USA)
      17. Washington Whips (USA)
  • 1969 -
    • Kansas City Athletics move to Oakland
    • Minnesota Pipers move back to Pittsburgh
    • Oakland Oaks move to Washington, D.C. and become the Caps
    • Houston Mavericks move to North Carolina and become the Carolina Cougars
    • Salt Lake Golden Eagles (WHL) are established
    • Twelve charter NASL teams cease operations, leaving the Atlanta Chiefs, Baltimore Bays (who also cease operations after the season), Dallas Tornado, Kansas City Spurs, and St. Louis Stars
  • 1970 -

      1. Baltimore Colts (AFC East)
      2. Boston Patriots (AFC East)
      3. Buffalo Bills (AFC East)

      8. Kansas City Chiefs (AFC West)
      9. Miami Dolphins (AFC East)
      10. New York Jets (AFC East)
      11. Oakland Raiders (AFC West)
      12. Pittsburgh Steelers (AFC Central)
      13. San Diego Chargers (AFC West)
      The other 13 NFL teams forn the new National Football Conference:
      1. Atlanta Falcons (NFC West)
      2. Chicago Bears (NFC Central)
      3. Dallas Cowboys (NFC East)
      4. Detroit Lions (NFC Central)
      5. Green Bay Packers (NFC Central)
      6. Los Angeles Rams (NFC West)
      7. Minnesota Vikings (NFC Central)
      8. New Orleans Saints (NFC West)
      9. New York Giants (NFC East)
      10. Philadelphia Eagles (NFC East)
      11. St. Louis Cardinals (NFC East)
      12. San Francisco 49ers (NFC West)
      13. Washington Redskins (NFC East)
    • Buffalo Braves (NBA), Cleveland Cavaliers (NBA), and Portland Trail Blazers (NBA) are established
    • Washington Caps move to Norfolk, Virginia and become the Squires
    • Los Angeles Stars move to Salt Lake City, Utah
    • New Orleans Buccaneers move to Memphis and become the Pros
    • Pittsburgh Pipers become the Condors
    • Buffalo Sabres (displacing the AHL Bisons) and Vancouver Canucks (from the WHL) join the NHL
    • Rochester Lancers and Washington Darts join the NASL from the American Soccer League
    • Kansas City Spurs cease operations after the season
  • 1971 -
    • San Diego Rockets move to Houston
    • San Francisco Warriors move across the San Francisco Bay to Oakland and become the Golden State Warriors
    • Montreal Olympique (NASL), New York Cosmos (NASL), and Toronto Blizzard (NASL) are established
  • 1972 -
    • Dallas Rangers move to Arlington in the Dallas-Fort Worth metroplex and become the Texas Rangers
    • Cincinnati Royals move to Kansas City and become the Kings
    • Pittsburgh Condors and Miami Floridians cease operations
    • San Diego Conquistadors (ABA) are established
    • Memphis Pros become the Tams
    • World Hockey Association is established with twelve teams:
      1. Chicago Cougars

  • 1973 -

    • Ottawa Nationals move to Toronto and become the Toros
    • Atlanta Chiefs become the Apollos but cease operations after the season
    • Miami Gatos become the Toros
    • Philadelphia Atoms (NASL) are established
    • Montreal Olympique cease operations after the season
  • 1974 -

    • San Diego Conquistadors become the Sails but fold in November

    • California Golden Seals move to Cleveland and become the Barons

  • 1977 -

    • Miami Toros move to Fort Lauderdale and become the Strikers

  • 1978 -
