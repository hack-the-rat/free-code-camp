Professional Sports

A history of the Big 6 sports leagues in North America

Here's a timeline of professional sports leagues in the United States. Let us begin:

  • 1869 - Hamilton Tigers are established
  • 1870 - Chicago Cubs are established
  • 1871 - Boston Braves are established
  • 1873 - Toronto Argonauts are established
  • 1876 - National League is established (Chicago Cubs and Boston Braves are the only charter members still in existence); Ottawa Redblacks are established
  • 1882 - American Association is established with six teams: Baltimore Orioles, Cincinnati Reds, Louisville Colonels, Philadelphia Athletics, Pittsburgh Pirates, St. Louis Browns
  • 1883 - New York Giants and Philadelphia Phillies (both NL) are established
  • 1884 - Brooklyn Dodgers (AA) are established
  • 1887 - Cleveland Spiders (AA) are established; Pittsburgh Pirates defect to National League
  • 1889 - Cleveland Spiders defect to the National League
  • 1890 - Broolyn Dodgers and Cincinnati Reds defect to National League; Philadelphia Athletics fold
  • 1891 - Washington Senators (AA) are established; American Association is disbanded
  • 1892 - Baltimore Orioles, St. Louis Cardinals (formerly Browns), and Washington Senators join the National League
  • 1899 - Baltimore Orioles, Cleveland Spiders, Louisville Colonels, and Washington Senators fold
  • 1900 - National League "Classic Eight" lineup of teams is established: Boston Braves, Brooklyn Dodgers, Chicago Cubs, Cincinnati Reds, New York Giants, Philadelphia Phillies, Pittsburgh Pirates, and St. Louis Cardinals
  • 1901 - American League is established with eight teams: Baltimore Orioles, Boston Red Sox, Chicago White Sox, Cleveland Indians, Detroit Tigers, Milwaukee Brewers, Philadelphia Athletics, and Washington Senators
  • 1902 - Milwaukee Brewers move to St. Louis and become the Browns
  • 1903 - Baltimore Orioles fold and are replaced by the New York Yankees; Pacific Coast League is established
  • 1910 - Montreal Canadiens and Regina Roughriders are established; National Hockey Association is established
  • 1917 - National Hockey League is established with charter teams from NHA: Montreal Canadiens, Ottawa Senators, Toronto Arenas, and Montreal Wanderers (who immediately fold)
  • 1919 - Quebec Bulldogs are reactivated but fold after the season; Toronto Arenas become the St. Patricks
  • 1920 - Quebec Bulldogs move to Hamilton and become the Tigers; National Football League is established (only two original teams remain: Chicago Cardinals, Decatur Staleys; another team is descended from the charter team Dayton Triangles)
  • 1921- Decatur Staleys move to Chicago and become the Bears the next year; Green Bay Packers join NFL
  • 1923 - Toronto St. Patricks become the Maple Leafs
  • 1924 - Boston Bruins and Montreal Maroons (both NHL) created as expansion teams; Hamilton Tigers are expelled
  • 1925 - New York Americans (NHL), New York Giants (NFL), and Pittsburgh Pirates (NHL) created as expansion teams
  • 1926 - Chicago Blackhawks, Detroit Red Wings, and New York Rangers (all NHL) created as expansion teams
  • 1930 - Portsmouth Spartans (NFL) created as expansion team; Pittsburgh Pirates (NHL) move to Philadelphia and become the Quakers but last one season before suspending operations; Winnipeg Blue Bombers are established; Dayton Triangles move to Brooklyn and become the Dodgers
  • 1932 - Boston Redskins (NFL) created as expansion team
  • 1933 - Philadelphia Eagles and Pittsburgh Steelers (both NFL) created as expansion teams
  • 1934 - Portsmouth Spartans move to Detroit and become the Lions; Ottawa Senators move to St. Louis and become the Eagles but are disbanded after the season
  • 1937 - Boston Redskins move to Washington; Cleveland Rams join NFL from AFL II
  • 1938 - Montreal Maroons suspend operations (they would never return on ice)
  • 1941 - Hamilton Wildcats are established
  • 1942 - National Hockey League "Original Six" lineup of teams is established following the suspension of the New York Americans: Boston Bruins, Chicago Blackhawks, Detroit Red Wings, Montreal Canadiens, New York Rangers, and Toronto Maple Leafs
  • 1944 - Boston Yanks (NFL) established as expansion team
  • 1945 - Calgary Stampeders are established; Boston Yanks and Brooklyn Dodgers merge
  • 1946 - Cleveland Rams move to Los Angeles; Montreal Alouettes are established; Basketball Association of America is established (notable charter teams: Boston Celtics, Chicago Stags, New York Knicks, Philadelphia Warriors, St. Louis Bombers, and Washington Capitols); All-America Football Conference is established with eight teams (Brooklyn Dodgers, Buffalo Bisons/Bills, Cleveland Browns, Los Angeles Dons, Miami Seahawks (who move to Baltimore the next year and become the Colts), New York Yankees (who later merge with the aforementioned Dodgers), and San Francisco 49ers)
  • 1947 - Baltimore Bullets join BAA from American Basketball League
  • 1948 - Fort Wayne Pistons, Minneapolis Lakers, and Rochester Royals join BAA from National Basketball League
  • 1949 - National Basketball Association is established from BAA-NBL merger which includes the expansion Indianapolis Olympians and eight current teams: Boston Celtics, Fort Wayne Pistons, Minneapolis Lakers, New York Knicks, Philadelphia Warriors, Rochester Royals, Syracuse Nationals, and Tri-Cities Blackhawks; Chicago Stags and St. Louis Bombers fold after the season; Boston Yanks move to New York City; Edmonton Eskimos are established
  • 1950 - Hamilton Tigers and Wildcats merge to become the Hamilton Tiger-Cats; Cleveland Browns and San Francisco 49ers join NFL from AAFC; Baltimore Colts join as well but fold after the season; Washington Capitols (NBA) fold midway through the season
  • 1951 - Tri-Cities Blackhawks move to Milwaukee and become the Hawks; New York Yanks fold after the season
  • 1952 - Dallas Texans are established from remains of New York Yanks but fold after the season; Pacific Coast League achieves "Open" classification: classic teams include Hollywood Stars, Los Angeles Angels, Oakland Oaks (who later move to Vancouver and become the Mounties), Portland Beavers, Sacramento Solons, San Diego Padres, San Francisco Seals, and Seattle Rainiers; Western Hockey League is established and would include: Calgary Stampeders (1952–1963), Denver Spurs (1968–1974), Edmonton Flyers (1952–1963), Los Angeles Blades (1961–1967), New Westminster Royals (1952–1959), Phoenix Roadrunners (1967–1974), Portland Buckaroos (1960–1974), Salt Lake Golden Eagles (1969–1974), San Diego Gulls (1966–1974), San Francisco Seals (1961–1967), Saskatoon Quakers (1952–1959), Seattle Totems (1952–1974), Spokane Comets (1958–1963), Vancouver Canucks (1952–1970), Victoria Cougars (1952–1961), and Winnipeg Warriors (1955–1961)
  • 1953 - Boston Braves move to Milwaukee; Baltimore Colts (NFL) are established from remains of Dallas Texans; Indianapolis Olymipans fold after the season
  • 1954 - St. Louis Browns move to Baltimore and become the Orioles; Baltimore Bullets fold midway into the season; BC Lions are established
  • 1955- Philadelphia Athletics move to Kansas City; Milwaukee Hawks move to St. Louis
  • 1957 - Rochester Royals move to Cincinnati; Fort Wayne Pistons move to Detroit
  • 1958 - New York Giants move to San Francisco; Brooklyn Dodgers move to Los Angeles; the move to California by the Giants and Dodgers effectively kill the PCL as a potential major league; Canadian Football League is established with nine teams: BC Lions, Calgary Stampeders, Edmonton Eskimos, Hamilton Tiger-Cats, Montreal Alouettes, Ottawa Rough Riders, Saskatchewan Roughriders, Toronto Argonauts, and Winnipeg Blue Bombers
  • 1960 - Chicago Cardinals move to St. Louis; Dallas Cowboys (NFL) created as expansion teams; Minneapolis Lakers move to Los Angeles; American Football League is established with eight teams: Boston Patriots, Buffalo Bills, Dallas Texans, Denver Broncos, Houston Oilers, Los Angeles Chargers, New York Jets, and Oakland Raiders
  • 1961 - Los Angeles Chargers move to San Diego; Chicago Packers/Zephyrs (NBA) and Minnesota Vikings (NFL) created as expansion teams; Continental League established with eight teams: Atlanta Shamrocks, Buffalo Bisons, Dallas Rangers, Denver Bears, Houston Astros, Minnesota Twins, New York Mets, and Toronto Maple Leafs
  • 1962 - Philadelphia Warriors move to San Francisco
  • 1963 - Syracuse Nationals move to Philadelphia and become the 76ers; Chicago Packers/Zephyrs move to Baltimore and become the Bullets; Dallas Texans move to Kansas City and become the Chiefs
  • 1965 - Denver Bears move to Anaheim and become the California Angels
  • 1966 - Chicago Bulls (NBA), Atlanta Falcons (NFL), and Miami Dolphins (AFL) created as expansion teams
  • 1967 - Seattle Supersonics (NBA), San Diego Rockets (NBA), and New Orleans Saints (NFL) created as expansion teams; American Basketball Association is established with eleven teams: Anaheim Amigos, Dallas Chaparrals, Denver Rockets; Houston Mavericks, Indiana Pacers, Kentucky Colonels, Minnesota Muskies, New Orleans Buccaneers, New York Nets, Oakland Oaks, and Pittsburgh Pipers; National Hockey League "Expansion Six" is established with six expansion teams: California Golden Seals, Los Angeles Kings, Minnesota North Stars, Philadelphia Flyers, Pittsburgh Penguins, and St. Louis Blues
  • 1968 - St. Louis Hawks move to Atlanta; Anaheim Amigos move to Los Angeles and become the Stars; Minnesota Muskies move to Miami and become the Floridians; Pittsburgh Pipers move to Minneapolis-St. Paul Cincinnati Bengals (AFL), Milwaukee Bucks (NBA), and Phoenix Suns (NBA) created as expansion teams
  • 1969 - Kansas City Athletics move to Oakland; Minnesota Pipers move back to Pittsburgh and become the Condors the following year; Houston Mavericks move to Greensboro and become the Carolina Cougars; Oakland Oaks move to Washington and become the Caps; CL-AL-NL merger; Major League Baseball is established with the American and National Leagues; eight teams absorbed from the CL: New York Mets (NL), Houston Astros (NL), (Anaheim) California Angels (AL), Minnesota Twins (AL), Atlanta Braves (NL), Buffalo Bisons (NL), (Dallas) Texas Rangers (AL), and Toronto Blue Jays (AL)
  • 1970 - Buffalo Braves, (NBA), Buffalo Sabres (NHL), Cleveland Cavaliers (NBA), Portland Trail Blazers (NBA), and Vancouver Canucks (NHL) created as expansion teams; Los Angeles Stars move to Salt Lake City and become the Utah Stars; New Orleans Buccaneers move to Memphis and become the Sounds; Washington Caps move to Norfolk and become the Virginia Squires; AFL-NFL merger; American Football Conference and National Football Conference established with thirteen teams each: (AFC: Baltimore Colts, Boston Patriots, Buffalo Bills, Miami Dolphins, New York Jets, Cincinnati Bengals, Cleveland Browns, Houston Oilers, Pittsburgh Steelers, Denver Broncos, Kansas City Chiefs, Oakland Raiders, and San Diego Chargers), (NFC: Dallas Cowboys, New York Giants, Philadelphia Eagles, St. Louis Cardinals, Washington Redskins, Chicago Bears, Detroit Lions, Green Bay Packers, Minnesota Vikings, Atlanta Falcons, Los Angeles Rams, New Orleans Saints, and San Francisco 49ers)
  • 1971 - San Diego Rockets move to Houston
  • 1972 - Miami Floridians and Pittsburgh Condors fold following the season and are replaced by the expansion San Diego Conquistadors; Atlanta Flames and New York Islanders (both NHL) created as expansion teams; World Hockey Association is established with twelve teams: Chicago Cougars, Cleveland Crusaders, Edmonton Oilers, Los Angeles Sharks, Minnesota Fighting Saints, New York Raiders, Ottawa Nationals, Philadelphia Blazers, Quebec Nordiques, and Winnipeg Jets; Cincinnati Royals move to Kansas City and become the Kings
  • 1973 - Baltimore Bullets move to Washington; Ottawa Nationals move to Toronto and become the Toros; Philadelphia Blazers move to Vancouver; New York Raiders move to Cherry Hill and become the Jersey Knights; Dallas Chaparrals move to San Antonio and become the Spurs
  • 1974 - Indianapolis Racers (WHA), Kansas City Scouts (NHL), New Orleans Jazz (NBA), and Washington Capitals (NHL) created as expansion teams; Phoenix Roadrunners join WHA; Carolina Cougars move to St. Louis and become the Spirits; Jersey Knights move to San Diego and become the Mariners; Los Angeles Sharks move to Detroit and become the Michigan Stags
  • 1975 - Memphis Sounds move to Baltimore and become the Claws but fold before the season; Utah Stars fold early into the season; Michigan Stags move to Baltimore midway into the season and play out the rest of the year as the Blades before folding altogether; Vancouver Blazers move to Calgary and become the Cowboys; Cincinnati Stingers (WHA) created as expansion team; Denver Spurs join WHA but move to Ottawa to become the Civics before folding after two weeks
  • 1976
    • - Virginia Squires fold just before the ABA-NBA merger; Minnesota Fighting Saints fold midway into the season; Cleveland Crusaders move to Minneapolis-St. Paul and become the second incarnation of the Fighting Saints, only to fold midway into the following season; six ABA teams absorbed to NBA: Denver Nuggets, Indiana Pacers, Kentucky Colonels, New Jersey Nets, San Antonio Spurs, and Spirits of St. Louis; Kansas City Scouts move to Denver and become the Colorado Rockies; Toronto Toros move to Birmingham and become the Bulls; California Golden Seals move to Cleveland and become the Barons; Seattle Seahawks and Tampa Bay Buccaneers (both NFC) created as expansion teams; Birmingham Vulcans and Memphis Grizzlies (both AFC) join NFL from WFL
  • 1977
    • - Kansas City Royals (MLB AL), Montreal Expos (MLB NL), San Diego Padres (MLB NL), Seattle Mariners (MLB AL), and Pittsburgh Ironmen (NBA) created as expansion teams; Calgary Cowboys, Phoenix Roadrunners, and San Diego Mariners fold following the season
  • 1978
    • - Cleveland Barons merge with Minnesota North Stars; Buffalo Braves move to San Diego and become the Clippers
  • 1979
    • - New Orleans Jazz move to Salt Lake City and become the Utah Jazz; NHL-WHA merger; six WHA teams absorbed into NHL: Edmonton Oilers, Cincinnati Stingers, Houston Aeros, Hartford Whalers, Quebec Nordiques, and Winnipeg Jets
  • 1980 - Dallas Mavericks (NBA) created as expansion team; Atlanta Flames move to Calgary
  • 1982 - Oakland Raiders move to Los Angeles; Colorado Rockies move to Newark and become the New Jersey Devils;
